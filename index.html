Around the World in 72 Days...

In 1888 Nellie Bly, a journalist at the New York World suggested to her editor that she would take a trip around the world in an attempt to turn the fictional book, "Around the World in Eighty Days" into a reality for the first time. A year later on November 14 1889, at 9:30am her journey began. She set out from Jersey city on the Agusta Victoria and began her 24 899 mile journey. The map retraces her steps in attempt to show you what she would have seen in comparison to what you would see if you set out on the same journey today.

Hover over the numbers on the map, when a plane appears click to see what the journey would look like today, when a ship appears click to see what Nellie's journey would look like.
